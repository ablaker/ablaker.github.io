SPRING TERM

Girls Varsity
Crew

Head Coach: Mr. Shotwell

Assistant Coaches: Mr. Pratt, Mr. Lacasse

Captains: Eliza Ford, Katie Gilland

I was a novice rower coming to Taft and into this spring. I had done a week-long intensive camp over the summer which taught me the basic form of the stroke but for the most part, I was clueless on the first day. Luckily, I wasn't the only one. It's safe to say the first time I went on an erg was the most horrific experience I have ever had. I wanted to quit more than anything, but I persisted, and I am so glad I did.

Crew has been the most rewarding sport I have ever done. All your hard work is channeled into a singular race, a mere six to eight minutes in which you endure excruciating pain and discomfort. Rowing is a team sport like no other. Without a positive and cohesive boat, you cannot reach maximum boat speed. Not only must you be perfectly in sync physically, but you must push your teammates mentally; to compete, to challenge, and to extend their limits and capabilities.
